The Night Nell's Turned Into a 90's House Party

How a last-minute DJ set at Nell's Social in Bartonsville, PA became the blueprint for what the lounge does next.
Full Article (paste-ready)
Friday night I didn't have a plan. That's the part nobody believes when I tell this story. No flyer, no three-week rollout, no boosted post. I just had a DJ, a stack of 90's records worth of energy, and a room at Nell's that's usually built for cigars and conversation, not a dance floor. By 9 PM the lounge was full. By 10 it was loud in the way a room gets loud when people stop checking their phones. By midnight I had people I've never met before telling me it was the best night out they'd had in Monroe County in years. We're doing it again. Here's the business lesson, and it's not "throw more parties." It's this: I stopped guessing what my community wanted and I just asked them, and then I moved fast enough to actually deliver it. Owning a cigar lounge, running real estate, executive-producing a TV network — every one of those businesses has the same failure mode. You get so deep in your own plan that you stop listening to the room. I built Nell's around what I thought a premium cigar lounge should feel like. Turns out my patrons wanted something looser some nights — music, a reason to dress up, a night that wasn't about the cigar being the whole event. A last-minute 90's set is a small thing. It's also proof of concept. It told me the room can hold more than one identity without losing what makes it Nell's. That's the same instinct I use walking a listing in Pocono Summit or building out a TV development timeline — you build the frame, but you stay loose enough inside it to respond to what's actually in front of you. The plan is a starting point, not a cage. So this week I did something I don't do enough of — I asked the room directly. What do you actually want to see happen here on the weekends? The answer wasn't complicated. More upbeat music. A DJ, consistently, not as a surprise. Something to build a Friday around. And people wanted more than one lane — not everybody's coming out for the same reason on the same night. That's the operator lesson underneath the fun one: your best product research isn't a survey, it's a full room telling you the truth with their energy. I've built three brands off the same instinct — watch what people actually respond to, not what I assumed they'd respond to, and move on it before the moment cools off. A DJ set on a Friday isn't a pivot. It's data. And starting this week, we're acting on it. Bartonsville's about to get a lot louder on the weekends. Come find out for yourself. — Alexa Sanchez, PA License #RS326383, Smart Way America Realty
